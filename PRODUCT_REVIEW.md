# Product Review

## Strengths
- The app now communicates clearly that it is an educational screening tool rather than a diagnostic test.
- The wording is safer and more trustworthy for a first-time user, especially around symptoms, follow-up care, and the absence of prescription calculation.
- The flow is straightforward and easy to follow for a non-technical user, with one primary action per screen.
- The experience feels calm, low-pressure, and suitable for a quick self-check.
- The app avoids implying that it can determine an exact eyeglass power or "eye number."

## Weaknesses
- The screening remains a simplified approximation and should not be interpreted as a substitute for a professional eye exam.
- Some screens still depend on lighting, screen quality, and user interpretation, which can affect consistency.
- The results are useful for awareness and prompting follow-up care, but they are not precise enough for medical decision-making.
- A few educational concepts are still somewhat simplified for a highly technical audience, though they are generally understandable.

## Remaining limitations
- The app cannot diagnose eye disease, estimate a prescription, or replace a comprehensive examination.
- It does not assess conditions such as glaucoma, retinal disease, cataracts, or other serious eye problems in a definitive way.
- The experience is best used as an educational and awareness tool for prompting a professional exam when needed.
- Results may be affected by screen brightness, device quality, room lighting, and the user’s comfort level during the test.

## Production readiness
- The app is reasonably ready for public use as an educational vision screening tool.
- It is appropriate for helping users decide whether an eye exam may be worthwhile, not for diagnosing conditions or prescribing correction.
- The current wording and flow improvements have strengthened user trust and reduced misleading implications.
